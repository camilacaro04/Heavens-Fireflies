Glossary of terms

Astrology:The subject that studies the relationship between celestial bodies and human experience. This subject has many divisions; popular, medical, mundane, world, horary.

  • Popular astrology is frequently found in media sources; and due to its narrow focus, is discredited among serious astrologers.
  • Medical astrology seeks to identify ailments.
  • Mundane astrology explains natural events.
  • World astrology explains the fates of nations.
  • Horary astrology describes important future dates with which to make decisions.
  • Natal astrology describes the horoscope calculated at birth.

Native: The individual for whom the horoscope is calculated from birth information.

Geocentric: A conceptual framework whereby the Earth is central to the Solar system.

Heliocentric: A conceptual framework whereby the Sun is central to the Solar System.

Celestial Sphere: A conceptual sphere surrounding the Earth using projected geometry.

Ecliptic: The name given to the Sun's apparent path around the Earth during the year and labelled as such because lunar and solar eclipses occur when the Moon crosses this path.

Zodiac: A twelve-part division of the celestial sphere in the Ecliptic. The zodiac is most commonly used to describe the twelve zodiac signs.

Zodiac Sign: The name given to one twelfth division of the sky surrounding the Earth in a disc formation, for example Aries and Taurus are adjacent to each other. These are most popularly known as 'star sign'.

Planet: Any celestial body orbiting the Earth from a geocentric perspective, for example the Sun, Moon, or Pluto. These are also further divided into types of planets. For the purpose of this research, focus is solely on the 'Personal Planets'

Personal Planet: These are the celestial bodies which are the closest, fastest moving and are therefore considered to have an immediate impact on daily experience, whereas the farther 'Transpersonal Planets' are considered to impact whole generations of individuals due to slower and therefore longer transits (Pluto spends 20 years transiting a sign). The personal planets are: Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus and Mars.

House: The name given to one twelfth division of the horizon surrounding the Earth, in a disc formation, starting from a specific point related to the native's birth information. For example, the first and second houses are adjacent to each other, always beginning at the point the Sun rises on the horizon at the time of birth.

Modality: The division of the 12 signs into 3 groups; Cardinal, Fixed and Mutable. These divisions relate to shared qualities of being.

Element: The division of the 12 signs into 4 groups; Water, Earth, Air and Fire. These divisions relate to shared qualities of being.
