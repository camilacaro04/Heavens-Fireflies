What is astrology?

From a geocentric perspective, space surrounding the Earth is conceptually divided into a wide band of various sections, each referred to as zodiac signs. When a natal chart is calculated, the positions of the planets are recorded against this Zodiac Belt at the time of birth, effectively producing a map. All individuals will have each of the planets in their birth chart, though in different positions, as charted via their usual planetary cycles and orbits (Pellegrini, 1973). Astrologers refer to the individual in question as a native. Each planet, the Sun and Moon included, are posited to represent unique drives; for example, Mercury communicates, while Saturn restricts. If an individual's Mercury falls in the zodiac sign of Pisces, the native is posited to then communicate in a Piscean manner (Pellegrini, 1973). Each of the signs represent an attitude to life. In this case, the native is suggested to communicate in a discrete, understated and introverted way (Clarke et al., 1996). In summary, planets show what is happening, signs show how it is happening and houses show where it is happening.
